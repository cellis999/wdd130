Client Report

I answered the questions I thought were relevant because I did my own website.

HOW DID YOU DECIDE WHAT CONTENT TO USE FOR YOUR SITE?: When I decided to create a portfolio, I decided I wanted to be able to showcase all my work throughout my years of schooling. I still have some placeholder images in my website as I am still waiting to finish up some stuff and get some images of projects, but I wanted it to look as complete as possible.

HOW DID YOU COMMUNICATE WITH YOUR DEVELOPER?: I did my own website for this project, but I did get feedback from one of the other developers.

HOW WAS YOUR EXPERIENCE MANAGING WITH GITHUB?: GitHub wasn't a huge deal for me, as I have done large scale team projects with merge management, etc. No issues with pushing or pulling, and everything went smoothly.

WHAT WAS THE MOST CHALLENGING PART OF THIS PROJECT?: The most challenging part of the project for me had to be the dynamic programming. I have never done any dynamic programming so I definitely challenged myself with that. I also had a couple issues with pathing, but I figured that out.

WHAT DID YOU ENJOY MOST ABOUT THIS PROJECT?: I definitely enjoyed seeing the result. I am glad to see the work that I was able to put in come back as something special. I am excited to continue to build on this throughout the years.

WHAT WOULD I DO DIFFERENTLY?: I think I would spend more time creating a uniform feel to my project cards. I had different project cards on my home page then my hardware and software pages, so I ended up just copying the HTML I was injecting on the hardware page and putting it in my front page. I think I could've saved myself a lot of time if I had planned that from the beginning.

DO YOU FEEL MORE CONFIDENT ABOUT WORKING ON REAL WORLD PROJECTS AFTER THIS?: Yes! I learned a bunch of new skills. I think being able to figure out how JavaScript works and utilize it in my final project was a great experience that will benefit me in the workplace.
